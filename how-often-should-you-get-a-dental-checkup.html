How Often Should You Get a Dental Checkup?

Most people only visit a dentist when pain starts. That is exactly what leads to expensive, stressful treatment later. The right timing prevents that.

The simple answer

For most people, a dental checkup every 6 months is recommended. But if you already have pain, bleeding gums, or sensitivity, waiting 6 months is a mistake.

Why checkups matter

Dental problems don’t start with pain. They start small — plaque, minor decay, gum irritation. By the time pain comes, the damage is already worse.

You should come earlier if you have:

  • Tooth pain or sensitivity
  • Bleeding gums
  • Bad breath that won’t go away
  • A broken or chipped tooth

What happens during a checkup?

The dentist checks your teeth, gums, and overall oral condition, then tells you exactly what needs to be done — before things get worse.

Reality check

Most patients who delay treatment end up needing more complex procedures. Early visits are always cheaper, faster, and less painful.

Do not wait for pain

If something feels off, it already is. The earlier you act, the easier it is to fix.
