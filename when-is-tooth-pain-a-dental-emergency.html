Dental Emergencies

When is tooth pain a dental emergency?

Not all tooth pain is the same. Some pain can wait for a normal appointment, but some signs mean you should contact the clinic quickly.

Call Now WhatsApp Us

Tooth pain should not be ignored

Even mild tooth pain can be a warning sign. Waiting too long may allow the problem to worsen and can make treatment more difficult later.

When you should seek help quickly

  • Severe or throbbing pain that does not ease
  • Swelling around the tooth, gum, or face
  • Pain with fever or general illness
  • A broken or knocked tooth after trauma
  • Pain when chewing that is getting worse
  • Bleeding that does not stop normally

When pain may still need prompt review

Even if it is not a full emergency, tooth sensitivity, sharp pain with hot or cold food, gum tenderness, or repeated discomfort are still reasons to book early instead of waiting for the issue to grow.

What you should not do

Do not keep self-medicating for many days while hoping the problem disappears. Painkillers may reduce discomfort temporarily, but they do not solve the underlying dental issue.

What to do next

If you are unsure whether your tooth pain is urgent, the safest move is to call or WhatsApp the clinic, explain your symptoms clearly, and get guidance on the next step.
